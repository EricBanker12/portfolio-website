About Me

Initially, I majored in biology to pursue a career in research. However, I am now working in web and software development.

Prior to 2018, most of my programming experience came from my hobby of creating scripts and helper programs for video games in AutoHotkey and NodeJS. Towards the end of my enrollment at Southern Illinois University Edwardsville, I started focusing more on programming for data science in R and python, and supplementing my studies with coursera.org online courses.

But after losing my brother to cancer and feeling frustrations with heavy academic requirements and slow progression of research, I decided to look for work outside of medical research. This led me to attending Lambda School training in full stack web development.

Today I boast a wide range of technical skills such as my strong background with HTML, CSS, Javascript, Git, and Node, in addition to my broader background in R, Python, Java, C, and more. I want to use my array of technical skills and biology background to improve people's lives and the pursuit of science.
